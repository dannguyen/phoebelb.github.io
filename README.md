<p><i><b>Wilfred</b></i> is an American television comedy series which debuted on June 23, 2011, and is based on the Australian <a href="/wiki/SBS_One" title="SBS One">SBS One</a> <a href="/wiki/Wilfred_(Australian_TV_series)" title="Wilfred (Australian TV series)">Wilfred (Australian TV series)</a>. It stars <a href="/wiki/Elijah_Wood" title="Elijah Wood">Elijah Wood</a> and series co-creator <a href="/wiki/Jason_Gann" title="Jason Gann">Jason Gann</a>, reprising his role of the <a href="/wiki/Eponym" title="Eponym">eponymous</a> dog Wilfred. The series was adapted for the American television channel <a href="/wiki/FX_(TV_channel)" title="FX (TV channel)">FX</a> by <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Guy" title="Family Guy">Family Guy</a></i> veteran <a href="/wiki/David_Zuckerman" title="David Zuckerman">David Zuckerman</a>. Season 4 premiered on June 25, 2014. FX announced on October 2, 2013 that <i>Wilfred</i><span style="padding-left:0.1em;">'</span>s fourth season would be moving to <a href="/wiki/FXX" title="FXX">FXX</a>, and that it would be the show's final season.<sup id="cite_ref-season4-news_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-season4-news-1"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup></p>

<p>The show follows a young man named Ryan (<a href="/wiki/Elijah_Wood" title="Elijah Wood">Elijah Wood</a>) and his neighbor's dog Wilfred (<a href="/wiki/Jason_Gann" title="Jason Gann">Jason Gann</a>). In the opening episode, Ryan concocts a drug cocktail in order to commit suicide. After this failed attempt, Ryan's neighbor, Jenna (<a href="/wiki/Fiona_Gubelmann" title="Fiona Gubelmann">Fiona Gubelmann</a>), knocks on his door to ask him to look after Wilfred, whom Ryan sees and hears as a man in a dog costume.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilfred_(U.S._TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development">Development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilfred_(U.S._TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Wilfred</i> is based on the critically acclaimed Australian series <a href="/wiki/Wilfred_(Australian_TV_series)" title="Wilfred (Australian TV series)">of the same name</a> and was adapted for <a href="/wiki/FX_(TV_channel)" title="FX (TV channel)">FX</a> by <i><a href="/wiki/Family_Guy" title="Family Guy">Family Guy</a></i> veteran <a href="/wiki/David_Zuckerman" title="David Zuckerman">David Zuckerman</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-knox_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-knox-7"><span>[</span>7<span>]</span></a></sup> <i>Wilfred</i> is produced by FX Productions while the executive producers include: Zuckerman;<sup id="cite_ref-website_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-website-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ew_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ew-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lang_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lang-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Rich and Paul Frank; <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Kwatinetz" title="Jeff Kwatinetz">Jeff Kwatinetz</a>; and Joe and Ken Connor from the original Australian series. <i>Wilfred</i> co-creator <a href="/wiki/Jason_Gann" title="Jason Gann">Jason Gann</a> and <a href="/wiki/Randall_Einhorn" title="Randall Einhorn">Randall Einhorn</a> serve as co-executive producers. Einhorn directed 10 episodes of the first season and <a href="/wiki/Victor_Nelli,_Jr." title="Victor Nelli, Jr.">Victor Nelli, Jr.</a> directed three.<sup id="cite_ref-website_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-website-8"><span>[</span>8<span>]</span></a></sup> The pilot episode was filmed in summer 2010,<sup id="cite_ref-ew_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ew-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lang_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lang-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> written by Zuckerman, and directed by Einhorn.<sup id="cite_ref-lang_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lang-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Unlike the Australian version—which concentrated equally on Wilfred, his owner, and her boyfriend—the American version is presented as a <a href="/wiki/Buddy_film" title="Buddy film">buddy comedy</a> between Wilfred and Ryan.<sup id="cite_ref-curtis_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-curtis-11"><span>[</span>11<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Casting">Casting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilfred_(U.S._TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Casting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The casting of <a href="/wiki/Elijah_Wood" title="Elijah Wood">Elijah Wood</a> as Ryan was announced on June 29, 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-ew_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ew-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lang_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lang-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> Ryan is described as "an introverted and troubled young man struggling unsuccessfully to make his way in the world until he forms a unique friendship with Wilfred, his neighbor's canine pet."<sup id="cite_ref-website-ryan_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-website-ryan-12"><span>[</span>12<span>]</span></a></sup> Series co-creator Jason Gann also reprises his role of the eponymous dog Wilfred, a character described by Zuckerman as being a mixed breed dog who is "part <a href="/wiki/Labrador_Retriever" title="Labrador Retriever">Labrador Retriever</a> and part <a href="/wiki/Russell_Crowe" title="Russell Crowe">Russell Crowe</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Binge_drinking" title="Binge drinking">bender</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-ew_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ew-9"><span>[</span>9<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lang_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lang-10"><span>[</span>10<span>]</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fiona_Gubelmann" title="Fiona Gubelmann">Fiona Gubelmann</a> stars as Jenna, Wilfred's owner and Ryan's next-door neighbor, who works as a local news producer.<sup id="cite_ref-website-jenna_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-website-jenna-13"><span>[</span>13<span>]</span></a></sup> Dorian Brown was then cast as Kristen, Ryan's controlling and condescending older sister.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span>[</span>14<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilfred_(U.S._TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Wilfred</i> is shot using a <a href="/wiki/DSLR" title="DSLR" class="mw-redirect">DSLR</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Canon_7D" title="Canon 7D" class="mw-redirect">Canon 7D</a>, using a <a href="/wiki/Multiple-camera_setup" title="Multiple-camera setup">three camera setup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span>[</span>15<span>]</span></a></sup> The show is now shot with a <a href="/wiki/DSLR" title="DSLR" class="mw-redirect">DSLR</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Nikon_D800" title="Nikon D800">Nikon D800</a>. <sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span>[</span>16<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Wilfred_(U.S._TV_series)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Critical reception of the US adaptation of <i>Wilfred</i> was comparable to that of the original Australian series, with generally positive reviews. <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">Review aggregator</a> website <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> gives the series an average of 67 out of 100. Curt Wagner, writing in <i>Redeye (<a href="/wiki/The_Chicago_Tribune" title="The Chicago Tribune" class="mw-redirect">The Chicago Tribune</a>)</i>, said "Stuffed with absurd situations and piles of bad taste, <i>Wilfred</i> is the strangest new show on TV. And the funniest."<sup id="cite_ref-redeye_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-redeye-17"><span>[</span>17<span>]</span></a></sup> David Wiegand, in the <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco_Chronicle" title="San Francisco Chronicle">San Francisco Chronicle</a>, said "<i>Wilfred</i> works on many levels, something that may not become apparent until after you stop laughing."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span>[</span>18<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>Some of the less positive reviews included Mike Hale, who wrote in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>: "Some shows aspire to cult status; this one goes straight there, practically bypassing the need to be broadcast at all," but concluded: "Gann’s bits of doggie business ... are reliably humorous, but beyond that the show doesn’t offer a lot of bark or bite."<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-19"><span>[</span>19<span>]</span></a></sup> Tom Gliatto of <a href="/wiki/Weekly" title="Weekly" class="mw-redirect">Weekly</a> also gave a less positive review, saying "The show is <i><a href="/wiki/The_Odd_Couple" title="The Odd Couple">The Odd Couple</a></i> redefined by psychosis and whimsy. I'm not wagging my tail."<sup id="cite_ref-_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note--20"><span>[</span>20<span>]</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span>[</span>21<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
<p>The series premiere hit a positive note with viewers as it became the highest-ranking debut sitcom ever for FX Networks. It continued to remain in the top 10 shows for Thursday night cable television throughout the first season. It was picked up for a second season on August 6, 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span>[</span>22<span>]</span></a></sup> The second season aired from June 21 to September 20, 2012. On October 31, 2012, <i>Wilfred</i> was renewed for Season 3,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span>[</span>23<span>]</span></a></sup> which aired from June 20 to September 5, 2013. On October 2, <i>Wilfred</i> was renewed for a fourth and final season<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span>[</span>24<span>]</span></a></sup> which aired from June 25 to August 13, 2014.</p>
